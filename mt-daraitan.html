Mt. Daraitan & Tinipak River

  • Mount Daraitan & Tinipak River

    Mount Daraitan and Tinipak River in Rizal Province are a half-day drive from Metro Manila and provide for a fantastic weekend vacation for the adventurous wishing to get away from their four walls and enjoy a nice trekking workout. This schedule, set smack dab in the middle of nature, can last a day or two. Prepare your hiking gear and a camera to record vistas of the mountain ranges, and prepare to reward yourself with a plunge in the limestone-banked river after your hike.

  • Climb that is well worth it for the vistas.

    The ascent to Mount Daraitan's top, 739 meters above sea level, is worthwhile for the vista of rolling clouds and mountains underneath you. You may reach here by taking either a lengthy, milder path or a short, steeper track to the top. Mountain climbers who are new to the sport should begin with the former, which will take them to the summit in around 4 hours. Plan about 6-8 hours for the full climb both ways, and wear strong shoes for the muddy and rocky terrain.

  • Heart Peak

    If you need a brief break, little booths along the route to the summit serve food and drinks. Request that your guide take you to the Heart Peak on the way to the top. When you reach the peak, look north for a glimpse of the Sierra Madre range, which is 200 kilometers away if the weather is clear.

  • Tinipak River

    The rock formations along the river are incredible and unlike anything I've ever seen. Many of these rocks have a considerably smoother look than the normal stiff rock formations in this area. They are, nevertheless, equally magnificent. The color of the river's water is likewise distinctive. It has an emerald/turquoise hue and appears to have been colored artificially. With the many colors of the river and the wonderful rock formations along the route, this is some of the most spectacular natural beauty I've seen in the Philippines.

  • Underground Cave

    After around 90 minutes, you will reach the river bridge that leads to the underground cave. This is a bamboo bridge that connects you to a slew of limestone rocks that you must carefully navigate in order to reach the cave's entrance. This isn't too tough, but be careful because the rocks are pretty slippery. From there, you return to the entrance and down the bamboo ladder into the cave. This is barely a few meters deep and requires the use of headlights.

  • Camping Ground

    Choose from Tinipak Camping Ground, which has an open-air village-style restaurant, The Private Camp: Daraitan, which has kubo-style raised huts, campfires, and eating spaces, Camp Tabing Ilog, which allows you to sleep in tent-shaped huts or pitch your own tents beside the river, and numerous more. We couldn't resist the urge to bring our own s'mores kits for the fire.

Quick Tip

Because of the stones that you may face along the trip, it is recommended that you wear appropriate hiking boots/shoes. The route was typically pretty slick, so you'll need strong traction on your footwear and extreme caution along the way. I would also advise you to use gloves to protect your hands.
