Nagpatong Rock Formation

  • Nagpatong Rock

    Nagpatong Rock is made up of two massive limestone cliffs that face one other. These towers appear to be formed of layers of massive pieces of rock piled on top of each other, hence the name Nagpatong, derived from the Tagalog word "patong," which means "to stack on top." However, only nature knows how these rock formations were developed.
    The view of Nagpatong Rock with a flat top that is generally captured from the other rock formation is the most photographed. Your guide will assist you to the flat top before proceeding to the other rock formation to capture the photo.

  • Hike that is well worth it for the view.

    The flat top, where a whole group may snap shots, is the most famous site here. Guides are trained to snap photographs, so after guiding hikers, they proceed to another nearby rock formation to photograph you.
    It is essential to take a solo shot, and there is time provided for you to completely enjoy the occasion. It's no surprise that it takes a bit longer to record the incredible experiences.

  • Long Trek

    It is unclear how Nagpatong Rock came to be. There is a popular belief that, like other areas in the Philippines, it was originally underwater before the sea level dropped.
    Nagpatong Rock is suitable for inexperienced climbers. The journey to the big tower normally takes around an hour.

Quick Tips

  • Wear appropriate clothing. Also make sure you’re wearing durable sandals or shoes.
  • Bring at least 1 liter of water.
  • Camping is allowed at Mount Masungki’s Billboard (5 minutes to summit)
