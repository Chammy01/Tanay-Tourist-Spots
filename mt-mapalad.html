Mt. Mapalad

  • Hiking Mt. Mapalad

    Mt. Mapalad is located in Tanay, Rizal, perfect destination for mountaineers at all levels. With 3/9 level of difficulty, this mountain is best for a dayhike adventure that is suitable for beginners. The trail offers beautiful scenery. At the summit, you can find these two instagrammable wooden palm structures with an impressive view of mountain range and sunrise.

  • About Mt. Apalad

    The term mapalad literally means "privileged" or "lucky" in Filipino. Mt. Mapalad has a tale or myth about how it came to be. However, according to guides, Mt. Mapalad got its name from a shrub that looks like the palm of a hand and may be found in the vicinity. The word palad literally means "palm."

  • Amazing View

    At the summit, you’ll be rewarded with an amazing view that will ease all your short breaths. The Sierra Madre mountain range, beautiful sunrise, and sometimes sea of clouds on a perfect weather condition. Aside from the scenic landscape, the summit also have these two (2) famous wooden palm structures where you can take a solo or group photo. Both structures are aesthetically pleasing and strategically place on a spot with a picturesque background.

Rules and FAQs

  • Don’t vandalize on trees and rock formations. Also, respect the wildlife. If you see wild animals, let them be. Don’t provoke them.
  • Pack light, bring only essentials like water, trail snacks, extra lightweight clothing, toiletries, and packed lunch (if needed).
  • Strictly follow Leave No Trace policy wherever you are. Don’t throw your trash anywhere. Place them in a proper garbage container or bring them with you if there’s no trash can in the area.
